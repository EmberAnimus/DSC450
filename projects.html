Projects

COVID-19 and How It Might Have Spread

This project analyzes the CDC's COVID-19 case surveillance database, which includes a large amount of data collected during the pandemic. Our study aims to show how COVID-19 control measures affect public health outcomes. The goal is to uncover correlations and casual relationships between different factors and COVID-19 related outcomes by throughouly examining all available data.

  • Data Wrangler: Kevin Arnold
  • Data Scientist: Brittany Roberts
  • Data Presenter: Sebastian Rolett

Project 2

The project aims to analyze the effects of inflation on family income and house selling prices in the United States. The data used in the project is sourced from the FRED Economic Data database, which is provided by the Federal Reserve Bank of St. Louis. The goal of the project is to understand how inflation and family income affect the affordability of housing for families.

  • Data Wrangler: Britanny Roberts
  • Data Scientist: Sebastian Rolett
  • Data Presenter: Kevin Arnold

Project 3

This project focuses on analyzing the United States SBA 7(a) loan data. This data was collected from the SBA website and contains information on loans and is recent as of September 30th, 2023. The aim of this project is to understand what charactaristics, risk factors, and lending practices lead to defaults or successful loans. In turn, the goal of the project is to understand the nauance of these factors to help the SBA make better lending decisions with better terms for qualified individuals.

  • Data Wrangler: Sebastian Rolett
  • Data Scientist: Kevin Arnold
  • Data Presenter: Brittany Roberts
